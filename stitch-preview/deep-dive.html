Execution Accounts: The Path to Abstraction

Current Externally Owned Accounts (EOAs) are fundamentally tied to the Elliptic Curve Digital Signature Algorithm (ECDSA). In a post-quantum landscape, Shor’s algorithm poses an existential threat to these keys.

COMPARATIVE ARCHITECTURE
Legacy EOA
Hard-coded ECDSA verification in the protocol layer. Inflexible and vulnerable.
PQC Abstraction
Smart-contract logic determines verification. Supports Winternitz OT-signatures.

The transition relies on Account Abstraction (AA) to decouple the verification logic from the core protocol, allowing users to rotate to quantum-resistant schemes without losing their on-chain identity.

Validators: BLS vs Lamport

Ethereum’s consensus layer currently utilizes BLS signatures for validator aggregation. While efficient, BLS relies on the hardness of pairings over elliptic curves. Research into Lamport Signatures and XMSS (eXtended Merkle Signature Scheme) is accelerating as a viable consensus-level replacement.

Rollups & ZK-STARK Systems

Layer 2 scaling solutions, particularly ZK-Rollups, provide a unique advantage. Unlike SNARKs, which often require a trusted setup and specific algebraic curves, **STARKs** (Scalable Transparent Argument of Knowledge) are inherently post-quantum secure as they rely on collision-resistant hash functions.

"The shift to STARK-based proof systems isn't just about scalability; it's the primary defense line for the Ethereum ecosystem's long-term integrity."

ERC-4337 Compatibility

The integration of "Paymasters" and "Bundlers" allows for quantum-resistant signatures to be verified off-chain before being committed, drastically reducing gas costs for complex PQC schemes.
